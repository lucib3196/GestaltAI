A car accelerates uniformly from rest to a speed of 30 m/s in 10 seconds. Calculate the total distance traveled by the car during this time.

The formula to calculate distance traveled with constant acceleration is given by:

\( d = \frac{1}{2} v_i t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \)

where \( v_i \) is the initial velocity (0 m/s), \( v_f \) is the final velocity, \( a \) is the acceleration, and \( t \) is the time. Solve for \( d \).
